# About

I know what you might be thinking: isn't it excessive for a one-dev team to produce a style guide? Normally, I'd agree. But as a new developer, I have a few reasons for doing so:

- portfolio reasons
Having a style guide shows a hiring manager that I can integrate design knowledge and brand purpose. These are useful skills for technical writing, not just UX.
- accessibility
A style guide lets users get to know my site's personality a little better. Plus, designers and developers can see the explicit logic.
- personal reasons
It's good for my sanity to have a record of design choices while building out the site. In the likely event that I choose to overhaul it in future, I'll be able to trace my reasoning.

Thanks to https://elementor.com/blog/web-design-style-guide/
